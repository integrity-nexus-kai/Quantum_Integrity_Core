\title{Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG):\\
A Spectral-Derived Effective Extension of General Relativity}

\author{Kai S. Dietrich}
\date{}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
We propose an effective extension of General Relativity motivated by spectral geometry. Starting from a spectral action based on the Dirac operator, we derive a low-energy truncation containing curvature-squared corrections. An additional logarithmic functional introduces nonlinear feedback at high curvature. The resulting field equations admit a scalar degree of freedom and modify high-curvature behavior while recovering General Relativity in the low-energy limit. Implications for black hole structure and cosmology are discussed, and testable deviations from standard predictions are outlined.
\end{abstract}
