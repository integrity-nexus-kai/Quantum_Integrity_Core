# What is Missing and Next Steps  
## Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG)

---

## 🇬🇧 English Version

### 1. Purpose

This document defines the concrete next steps required to advance TIG from an effective theoretical framework to a fully testable and physically established model.

It complements the limitations section by providing an actionable roadmap.

---

## 2. Highest Priority Tasks

### 2.1 Exact Derivation of Field Equations

Current status:

- effective equations established  
- spectral origin defined  

Missing:

- full variation of the action including  
  \[
  \mathcal{I}[D]
  \]
- explicit form of  
  \[
  \mathcal{I}_{\mu\nu}
  \]

  \]
- quasinormal mode spectrum  

---

### 2.3 Scalar Mode Coupling to Astrophysical Systems

Current status:

- coupling scaling known  

Missing:

- explicit waveform prediction  
- amplitude estimation

---

### 2.4 Parameter Constraints

Current status:

- parameter relations derived  

Missing:

  \[

  \]
- mapping to GW, cosmology, compact objects

## 5. Minimal Path to Strong Paper

To significantly strengthen the current work, the minimal required additions are:

1. one explicit perturbation calculation  
2. one quantitative observable prediction (amplitude level)  
3. one parameter constraint estimate  

---

## 6. Strategic Insight

TIG is already:

- mathematically structured  
- physically interpretable  
- partially testable  

However:

> its acceptance depends on converting structure into measurable predictions.

---

## 7. Summary

The core missing elements are:

- exact dynamics  
- full stability proof  
- observational constraints  

Once these are addressed, TIG can evolve from:

\[
\text{effective proposal}
\rightarrow
\text{testable gravitational model}
\]
