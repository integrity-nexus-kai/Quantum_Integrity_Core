# Hawking Sector

This sector investigates the relation between Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) and horizon thermodynamics.

The primary objective is not to replace Hawking radiation theory, but to explore whether structurally constrained horizon geometries may exhibit modified thermodynamic and evaporation behavior near critical TIG transitions.

---

## Scientific Focus

Current investigation areas include:

- horizon stability,
- surface gravity modification,
- critical evaporation behavior,
- metastable horizon configurations,
- relaxation dynamics near structural criticality,
- and possible spectral effects emerging from non-linear horizon transitions.

---

## Central TIG Perspective

In classical General Relativity, the event horizon is typically treated as a geometric boundary.

Within TIG, the horizon may instead represent a dynamically stabilized critical structure emerging from non-linear geometric self-organization.

This motivates investigation into whether Hawking-like radiation could partially reflect structural relaxation processes near critical horizon states.

---

## Current Status

This sector is exploratory and non-canonical.

No claims are made regarding:
- resolution of the black hole information paradox,
- replacement of quantum field theory,
- or derivation of a complete quantum gravity framework.

The purpose of this sector is strictly structural and thermodynamic analysis within the TIG framework.
