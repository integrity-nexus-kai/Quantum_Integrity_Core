## Transition Boundary Geometry in TIG Topology

Version 0.1

# 1. PURPOSE

This document defines the preliminary concept of critical surfaces within the Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) topology framework.

Critical surfaces describe the geometric boundaries separating distinct admissible and non-admissible regions of recursive geometric state space.

The objective is to formalize:

- topology-changing boundaries,
- admissibility transitions,
- critical root structures,
- and integrity-preserving geometric limits.

---

# 2. MOTIVATION

The TIG cubic equation

\[
x^3 - x^2 + \beta^3 = 0
\]

generates structurally distinct root regimes depending on the value of:

\[
\beta
\]

The discriminant

\[
\Delta = \beta^3(4 - 27\beta^3)
\]

defines the critical condition under which root structure changes.

This implies the existence of geometric transition boundaries within admissible state space.

These boundaries are referred to as critical surfaces.

---

# 3. BASIC DEFINITION

A critical surface is a geometric boundary separating distinct structural transition regimes.

Examples include boundaries between:

- horizon-supporting states,
- critical transition states,
- non-horizon compact states,
- and forbidden geometric configurations.

Critical surfaces define where admissibility structure changes qualitatively.

---

# 4. DISCRIMINANT SURFACE

The primary TIG critical surface is defined through:

\[
\Delta = 0
\]

or explicitly:

\[
\beta^3(4 - 27\beta^3)=0
\]

This surface corresponds to root degeneracy within the cubic equation.

At this boundary:

- multiple roots coalesce,
- topology changes occur,
- and transition structure becomes singular.

The discriminant surface therefore acts as the primary transition boundary of TIG topology.

---

# 5. ADMISSIBILITY BOUNDARY

Critical surfaces separate:

- admissible regions,
- from structurally forbidden regions.

A transition remains physically admissible only if recursive consistency is preserved while crossing the critical boundary.

Crossings violating structural integrity are interpreted as forbidden transitions.
