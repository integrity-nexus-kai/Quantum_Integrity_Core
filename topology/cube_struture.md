# CUBE STRUCTURE
## Structural Coordinate Model for TIG

Version 0.1  
Status: Foundational Draft

---

# 1. PURPOSE

This document defines the preliminary cube-structure interpretation of Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG).

The cube structure is not introduced as a physical object.

It is a structural coordinate model for organizing admissible transitions in the TIG solution space.

---

# 2. BASIC IDEA

TIG does not describe a single isolated horizon equation only.

It describes a constrained transition system.

The cubic relation

\[
x^3 - x^2 + \beta^3 = 0
\]

suggests that horizon formation depends on more than radial position alone.

A structural coordinate space is therefore required.

The cube structure provides a minimal organizational model for:

- horizon state,
- curvature regime,
- integrity condition,
- transition direction.

---

# 3. COORDINATE INTERPRETATION

A preliminary coordinate interpretation is:

| Coordinate | Meaning |
|---|---|
| \(X_1\) | radial horizon variable |
| \(X_2\) | curvature intensity regime |
| \(X_3\) | admissible correction / regularization scale |
| \(X_4\) | structural transition coordinate |

The first three coordinates define the visible structural configuration.

\(X_4\) defines the transition direction through the admissible solution space.

---

# 4. CUBE AS STATE ORGANIZER

The cube represents the finite structural state region in which admissible configurations can be compared.

Each point inside the cube corresponds to a possible structural state.

Not every state is physically admissible.

Admissibility depends on:

- root structure,
- curvature consistency,
- bounded transition behavior,
- integrity preservation,
- and discriminant constraints.

---

# 5. FACES, EDGES, AND TRANSITIONS

The cube structure allows transitions to be classified.

## Faces

Faces represent boundary regimes.

Examples include:

- low-curvature boundary,
- high-curvature boundary,
- horizon-supporting boundary,
- non-horizon boundary.

## Edges

Edges represent critical transition lines.

Examples include:

- transition toward root coalescence,
- transition toward horizon disappearance,
- transition toward regularized compact states.

## Interior

The interior represents admissible intermediate structural states.

---

# 6. ROLE OF THE DISCRIMINANT

The discriminant

\[
\Delta = \beta^3(4 - 27\beta^3)
\]

defines the critical boundary of the horizon equation.

The surface

\[
\Delta = 0
\]

separates distinct structural regimes.

In the cube model, this boundary acts as a transition surface between:

- horizon-admitting states,
- critical states,
- and non-horizon states.

---

# 7. ROLE OF \(X_4\)

\(X_4\) is the coordinate that moves the system between structurally distinct regions of the cube.

It is not a spatial direction.

It is a transition coordinate.

Its function is to parametrize:

- branch movement,
- root topology change,
- horizon loss,
- regularization transition,
- and structural admissibility.

---

# 8. INTEGRITY CONDITION

A state transition is integrity-preserving if it remains inside the admissible region of the cube.

A transition violates integrity if it crosses into a structurally forbidden regime without passing through an admissible critical boundary.

Thus, the cube structure provides a first model for defining allowed and forbidden geometric evolution.

---

# 9. RELATION TO TIG

The original TIG paper establishes the cubic horizon transition.

The cube structure extends this by asking how the transition is embedded in a larger structural coordinate space.

The cube does not replace the cubic equation.

It organizes the possible regimes generated by it.

---

# 10. CURRENT STATUS

This cube model is preliminary.

Open tasks include:

- defining exact coordinate domains,
- formalizing admissible regions,
- deriving transition operators,
- mapping the discriminant surface,
- relating cube regions to observable regimes,
- and connecting the model to SIR and quantum transition structures.

---

# 11. SUMMARY

In TIG:

- the cubic equation defines the transition law,
- the discriminant defines the critical boundary,
- \(X_4\) defines the structural transition direction,
- the cube defines the organizational state space.

The cube is therefore a structural model of admissible gravitational transition geometry.
