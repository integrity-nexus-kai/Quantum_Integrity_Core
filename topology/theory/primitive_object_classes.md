## Version

Draft 1.0  
Status: Structural Consolidation Phase  
Framework Status: Exploratory Mathematical Research

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the minimal structural object classes currently used within the TIG framework (Topological Integrity Gravity).

The purpose is not expansion of terminology.

The purpose is:

- reduction,
- structural clarification,
- dependency analysis,
- and separation between primitive and emergent structures.

This document establishes which concepts are currently treated as:

- primitive,
- derived,
- auxiliary,
- or exploratory.

---

# 2. Structural Principle

The TIG framework increasingly moves toward the hypothesis that:

> admissibility may act as a fundamental structural selection principle governing recursively consistent evolution.

This hypothesis remains exploratory.

No finalized physical interpretation currently exists.

---

# 3. Primitive Structures

Primitive structures are objects currently treated as irreducible within the framework.

They are not derived from other TIG objects.

---

## 3.1 Admissibility

### Status
Primitive

### Role

Admissibility denotes the existence condition for structurally allowed evolution.

It acts as a constraint principle limiting admissible recursive development.

### Current Interpretation

Admissibility is not currently defined as:

- a force,
- a field,
- or a physical particle.

It is treated as a structural consistency condition.

### Open Questions

- Can admissibility be formalized variationally?
- Can admissibility generate dynamics?
- Is admissibility globally or locally defined?
- Can admissibility be expressed covariantly?

---

## 3.2 Recursive Consistency

### Status
Primitive

### Role

Recursive consistency denotes preservation of structural coherence under recursive evolution.

It constrains recursive branching and admissibility-preserving development.

### Current Interpretation

Recursive consistency currently represents:

- bounded recursive continuation,
- non-divergent structural evolution,
- and branch compatibility.

No finalized operator formulation currently exists.

---

## 3.3 Bounded Recursive Evolution

### Status
Primitive

### Role

Recursive evolution is assumed to remain bounded under admissibility-preserving dynamics.

This acts as a stabilizing structural assumption.

### Current Interpretation

Boundedness currently remains heuristic.

No proof currently exists that all admissible recursive systems remain bounded.
