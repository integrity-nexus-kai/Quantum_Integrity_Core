

# Formal vs Heuristic Layers in TIG

## Version

Draft 1.0  
Status: Structural Consolidation Phase  
Framework Status: Exploratory Mathematical Research

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the current distinction between:

- formally defined structures,
- semi-formal structures,
- heuristic mappings,
- and speculative research directions

within the TIG framework (Topological Integrity Gravity).

The purpose is not expansion of interpretation.

The purpose is:

- structural clarification,
- prevention of interpretational drift,
- reduction of implicit overclaims,
- and preservation of mathematical discipline.

The framework now explicitly prioritizes:

> formal structure over interpretational narrative.

---

# 2. Structural Motivation

As theoretical frameworks grow, heuristic interpretation may unintentionally become treated as formal mathematical structure.

This creates risks including:

- semantic inflation,
- hidden assumptions,
- interpretational overreach,
- and mathematically unsupported conclusions.

The present document therefore attempts to establish:

> explicit separation between mathematical definition and heuristic interpretation.

---

# 3. Layer Classification System

The TIG framework currently distinguishes four structural interpretation layers.

---

# Layer F — Formal Structures

---

## Definition

Formal structures possess:

- explicit mathematical definition,
- operational role,
- internal consistency requirements,
- and defined structural usage.

---

## Requirements

Formal structures should possess:

- clear symbolic definition,
- definable dependency structure,
- and admissibility-compatible interpretation.

---

## Important Restriction

Formal does NOT imply experimentally verified.

Formal only denotes mathematically defined structure.

---

# Layer SF — Semi-Formal Structures

---

## Definition

Semi-formal structures possess:

- partial mathematical definition,
- structural motivation,
- and provisional operational interpretation.

However:

- mathematical closure remains incomplete.

---

## Typical Characteristics

Semi-formal structures often include:

- partially defined functionals,
- incomplete operators,
- provisional constraints,
- or reduced structural sectors.

---

# Layer H — Heuristic Structures

---

## Definition

Heuristic structures represent:

- analogies,
- structural intuition,
- conceptual mappings,
- or exploratory interpretational guidance.

---

## Important Restriction

Heuristic structures must NOT be treated as finalized mathematical objects.

---

# Layer S — Speculative Structures

---

## Definition

Speculative structures represent:

- open research directions,
- unconsolidated hypotheses,
- or mathematically undeveloped possibilities.

---

## Important Restriction

Speculative structures possess no finalized theoretical status.

# 5. Structural Interpretation Principle

The TIG framework now explicitly requires:

> interpretational claims must remain weaker than formal mathematical structure.

This principle attempts to prevent:

- heuristic inflation,
- semantic drift,
- and mathematically unsupported interpretation.

# 6. Hierarchical Protection Principle

Higher interpretational layers must never redefine lower formal layers.

In particular:

- heuristic mappings must not redefine primitive structure,
- speculative interpretation must not redefine formal definitions,
- and observational intuition must remain subordinate to mathematical consistency.
