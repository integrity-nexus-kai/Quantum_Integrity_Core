# 4. Trivial vs Non-Trivial Structure

---

# 4.1 Trivial Structure

A structure is considered trivial if it merely restates consistency without generating additional restriction.

Examples of potentially trivial formulations include:

- "consistent systems remain consistent,"
- "stable systems remain stable,"
- or unrestricted recursive reformulation without structural exclusion.

---

## Structural Risk

Purely tautological structure possesses:

- no predictive restriction,
- no exclusion power,
- and no emergent structural consequence.

---

# 4.2 Non-Trivial Structure

A structure becomes non-trivial if it generates:

- admissibility restrictions,
- forbidden recursive configurations,
- structural bifurcations,
- boundedness constraints,
- critical transitions,
- or emergent organization not imposed externally.

---

## Structural Criterion

Non-triviality requires:

> admissibility must eliminate or constrain otherwise possible recursive structures.
