# 1. Purpose

This document defines the current logical definition hierarchy of the TIG framework (Topological Integrity Gravity).

The purpose is not expansion of the framework.

The purpose is:

- prevention of circular definitions,
- clarification of derivational structure,
- reduction of hidden dependencies,
- and preservation of structural consistency.

The framework now explicitly prioritizes:

> hierarchical definitional consistency before theoretical expansion.

---

# 2. Structural Motivation

As theoretical structure grows, hidden recursive dependency risks increase.

Without explicit hierarchy, the framework may develop:

- circular derivations,
- semantic feedback loops,
- undefined primitive dependence,
- and unstable conceptual layering.

This document therefore attempts to establish:

> the current minimal logical ordering of TIG structures.

---

# 3. Hierarchical Principle

A higher-level structure may depend on lower-level structures.

A lower-level structure must NOT depend on higher-level emergent structures.

Formally:

\[
L_n \rightarrow L_{n+1}
\]

is admissible,
while inverse definitional dependence is prohibited.
