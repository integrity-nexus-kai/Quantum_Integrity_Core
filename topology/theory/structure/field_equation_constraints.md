# 1. Purpose

This document defines the current mathematical and structural constraints that any admissible TIG field equation should satisfy.

The objective is not to finalize a field equation.

The objective is:

- structural restriction,
- admissibility preservation,
- recursive consistency enforcement,
- and reduction of mathematically uncontrolled extensions.

This document therefore focuses on:

> admissibility constraints rather than phenomenological construction.

---

# 2. Structural Position

Within TIG, field equations are currently interpreted as:

> generators of admissibility-preserving recursive structural evolution.

This interpretation remains exploratory.

Field equations are subordinate to:

- admissibility,
- recursive consistency,
- bounded recursive evolution,
- and structural minimality.

# 5. Constraint C1 — General Relativity Compatibility

An admissible TIG field equation must recover the classical General Relativity limit in the appropriate low-recursion regime.

In particular:

\[
\beta \rightarrow 0
\]

should recover:

- Schwarzschild structure,
- classical relativistic geometry,
- and standard low-curvature behavior.

---

## Motivation

Failure to recover the GR limit invalidates the admissibility of the extension.
