# 1. Purpose

This document defines the current minimal axiom candidates of the TIG framework (Topological Integrity Gravity).

The purpose is not expansion of the framework.

The purpose is:

- reduction,
- primitive identification,
- dependency minimization,
- and structural consolidation.

The central objective is:

> determination of the smallest admissibility-preserving structural basis currently required by TIG.

This document therefore attempts to distinguish between:

- primitive structures,
- derived structures,
- auxiliary structures,
- and removable structures.

---

# 2. Structural Motivation

As the TIG framework evolves, semantic expansion becomes increasingly dangerous.

Without reduction, recursive terminology may become:

- redundant,
- circular,
- structurally undefined,
- or mathematically non-operational.

The present document therefore prioritizes:

> structural necessity over conceptual richness.

---

# 3. Minimal Structural Goal

The long-term objective is not maximal descriptive scope.

The objective is:

- minimal admissibility structure,
- maximal consistency,
- and recursive definitional closure.

The framework should become smaller,
not larger.
