# 1. Purpose

This document defines the current conceptual and provisional mathematical role of recursive consistency within the TIG framework (Topological Integrity Gravity).

The purpose is not to introduce new physical entities.

The purpose is:

- structural clarification,
- recursive admissibility formalization,
- consistency reduction,
- and separation between admissible and pathological recursive evolution.

Recursive consistency is currently treated as a primitive structural condition.

No finalized mathematical theory currently exists.

# 4. Recursive States

Let:

\[
S_n
\]

denote a recursive structural state at recursion level \(n\).

Recursive evolution is provisionally represented as:

\[
S_n \rightarrow S_{n+1}
\]

where recursive transitions may alter admissible structural configuration.

---

# 5. Recursive Consistency Principle

A recursive transition is considered admissible only if recursive structural coherence is preserved.

Formally:

\[
C(S_n, S_{n+1}) \geq 0
\]

where:

- \(C\) denotes a recursive consistency functional,
- and admissibility requires non-destructive recursive continuation.

---

# 6. Admissible Recursive Evolution

A recursive sequence:

\[
\{S_n\}
\]

is provisionally considered admissible if:

1. recursive consistency is preserved,
2. recursive evolution remains bounded,
3. recursive branch transitions remain structurally continuous,
4. and no pathological recursive collapse occurs.

---

\[

\]

for admissible recursive sequences.
