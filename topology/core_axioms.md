# PURPOSE

This document defines the minimal axiom set currently underlying the TIG topology framework.

The purpose of these axioms is not to complete the theory, but to establish a mathematically stable conceptual foundation for further formalization.

---

# AXIOM 1 — STRUCTURAL ADMISSIBILITY

Not every mathematically possible configuration is physically admissible.

A physical state must satisfy recursive structural consistency conditions.

Symbolically:

\[
S \in \mathcal{A}
\]

where:

- \(S\) denotes a candidate state,
- and \(\mathcal{A}\) denotes the admissible state space.

---

# AXIOM 2 — BOUNDED RECURSION

Recursive evolution is structurally bounded.

Admissible recursive transitions cannot diverge arbitrarily while preserving integrity.

Symbolically:

\[
\mathcal{R}(S_n) \rightarrow S_{n+1}
\]

with bounded recursive admissibility.

---

# AXIOM 3 — TOPOLOGICAL CONSISTENCY

Admissible evolution preserves generalized topological consistency.

Transitions that generate incompatible recursive topology are forbidden.

---

# AXIOM 4 — CRITICAL TRANSITION SURFACES

The recursive manifold contains critical surfaces separating distinct admissibility regions.

At such surfaces, recursive root structure changes qualitatively.

These transitions may correspond to generalized bifurcation boundaries.

---

# AXIOM 5 — STRUCTURAL STABILITY

Admissible states tend toward structurally stable recursive regions.

Stable attractor structures preserve recursive consistency under bounded perturbations.

---

# AXIOM 6 — INTEGRITY CONSERVATION

Recursive evolution approximately preserves generalized structural integrity.

Symbolically:

\[
\Delta \mathcal{I}_{\text{total}} \approx 0
\]

under admissible recursive evolution.

---

# AXIOM 7 — LIMITED PHYSICAL INTERPRETATION

The framework currently defines structural mathematical constraints, not a finalized physical ontology.

All physical interpretation remains provisional.

---

# AXIOM 8 — X4 AS STRUCTURAL COORDINATE

X4 is currently interpreted only as a structural transition coordinate governing recursive admissibility behavior.

It is not yet defined as a physical spacetime dimension.

---

# AXIOM 9 — NON-FINALITY

The framework remains incomplete.

All current formulations are exploratory and subject to revision.

---

# AXIOM 10 — CONSISTENCY PRIORITY

Internal consistency has priority over expansion speed.

No new structure should be introduced unless it improves explanatory coherence without contradiction.

---

# CURRENT ROLE OF THE AXIOMS

The current axioms provide:

- structural constraints,
- admissibility rules,
- recursive stability principles,
- and a minimal foundation for future formalization.

They do not yet constitute a complete physical theory.
