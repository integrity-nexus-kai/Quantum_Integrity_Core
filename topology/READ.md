# 1. PURPOSE

The topology layer defines the structural geometry framework underlying Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG).

While the original TIG paper establishes the cubic horizon transition equation, the topology layer investigates the deeper organization of admissible geometric state space.

The objective is to formalize:

- structural transition geometry,
- recursive geometric admissibility,
- critical topology boundaries,
- bounded state evolution,
- and integrity-preserving spacetime transitions.

---

# 2. RELATION TO TIG

The original TIG formulation introduces the cubic horizon relation:

\[
x^3 - x^2 + \beta^3 = 0
\]

This equation defines a critical transition between:

- horizon-supporting states,
- and non-horizon compact configurations.

The topology layer extends this framework by asking:

- how these transitions are structurally organized,
- which state evolutions are admissible,
- and how recursive consistency constrains geometric evolution.

# 3. CORE IDEAS

The topology framework is based on several foundational assumptions:

## Structural Admissibility

Not every mathematically possible geometric state is physically admissible.

Admissibility depends on:

- recursive consistency,
- bounded curvature evolution,
- topological continuity,
- and integrity preservation.

---

## Critical Transition Structure

The discriminant structure of the cubic equation defines critical topology boundaries.

These boundaries separate:

- stable horizon states,
- critical transition states,
- and forbidden geometric configurations.

---

## X4 Transition Coordinate

X4 is interpreted as a structural transition coordinate.

It parametrizes movement through admissible geometric state space.

X4 is not currently interpreted as:

- an ordinary spatial dimension,
- a compactified extra dimension,
- or a directly measurable coordinate.

Instead, X4 describes structural transition behavior between geometric regimes.

---

## Cube Structure

The cube structure acts as a structural state organizer.

It provides a preliminary coordinate framework for classifying:

- admissible regions,
- forbidden regions,
- transition surfaces,
- and topology-preserving evolution paths.
