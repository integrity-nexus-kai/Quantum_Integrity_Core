# TRANSITION OPERATORS
## Structural Evolution Operators in TIG Topology

Version 0.1  
Status: Foundational Draft

---

# 1. PURPOSE

This document introduces the preliminary concept of transition operators within the Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) topology framework.

Transition operators describe how admissible geometric states evolve across the root manifold.

The objective is not yet a fully formal operator algebra, but a structural framework for describing:

- admissible state evolution,
- branch transitions,
- critical topology movement,
- and recursive geometric consistency.

---

# 2. MOTIVATION

The TIG cubic equation defines admissible geometric states.

The root manifold organizes these states geometrically.

A remaining question is therefore:

> How does the system move between admissible states?

Transition operators are introduced to describe this movement.

---

# 3. BASIC INTERPRETATION

A transition operator acts on an admissible geometric state and produces another admissible state.

Symbolically:

\[
\mathcal{T}(S_i) \rightarrow S_j
\]

where:

- \(S_i\) is an initial admissible state,
- \(S_j\) is the resulting admissible state,
- and \(\mathcal{T}\) is a transition operator.

---

# 4. ADMISSIBILITY CONDITION

Not every mathematically possible transition is physically admissible.

An admissible operator must preserve:

- recursive consistency,
- bounded evolution,
- structural continuity,
- and integrity conditions.

Therefore:

\[
\mathcal{T}_{adm}: S_{adm} \rightarrow S_{adm}
\]

Forbidden operators produce transitions into structurally inconsistent regions.

---

# 5. OPERATOR TYPES

The following preliminary operator classes are proposed.

---

## 5.1 Branch Transition Operators

These operators move the system between solution branches of the cubic equation.

Examples include:

- horizon emergence,
- horizon disappearance,
- root coalescence,
- and compact-state transition.

---

## 5.2 Critical Transition Operators

Critical operators govern evolution near:

\[
\Delta = 0
\]

where the system approaches topology-changing boundaries.

These operators may control:

- bifurcation behavior,
- admissibility breakdown,
- and critical geometric transitions.

---

## 5.3 Recursive Stability Operators

These operators preserve recursive consistency during evolution.

Their role is to prevent transitions into forbidden structural configurations.

---

# 6. RELATION TO X4

X4 parametrizes movement generated by transition operators.

In this interpretation:

- the manifold defines structure,
- operators define evolution,
- and X4 defines transition direction.

Symbolically:

\[
\mathcal{T}_{X4}
\]

may describe admissible movement across structural state space.

---

# 7. RELATION TO ROOT MANIFOLD

The root manifold defines the geometric space of admissible states.

Transition operators define movement within this space.

Thus:

| Structure | Role |
|---|---|
| root manifold | geometric organization |
| transition operators | admissible evolution |

The manifold and operators together form the basis of TIG transition geometry.

---

# 8. TOPOLOGICAL INTERPRETATION

Transition operators may alter:

- branch connectivity,
- admissibility regions,
- critical transition behavior,
- and local topology structure.

However, topology-changing transitions remain constrained by integrity conditions.

The framework therefore differs from unconstrained geometric evolution.

---

# 9. RELATION TO GENERAL RELATIVITY

In standard General Relativity, spacetime evolution is primarily determined through metric dynamics.

Within TIG, evolution additionally depends on:

- structural admissibility,
- recursive consistency,
- and topology-preserving transition behavior.

Transition operators therefore represent a higher-order structural layer acting on geometric evolution.

---

# 10. POTENTIAL RELATION TO QUANTUM THEORY

Transition operators may later provide a bridge toward quantum-compatible state evolution.

Possible future directions include:

- discretized transition dynamics,
- bounded state evolution,
- topology-preserving quantum operators,
- and recursive admissibility conservation.

No complete quantum formalism is proposed at the present stage.

---

# 11. OPEN PROBLEMS

The following questions remain unresolved:

- explicit operator algebra,
- differentiability conditions,
- operator composition rules,
- tensor representation,
- commutation structure,
- transition metrics,
- and quantum interpretation.

These remain future work.

---

# 12. SUMMARY

Within TIG:

- the cubic equation defines admissible states,
- the discriminant defines critical boundaries,
- the root manifold defines geometric structure,
- and transition operators define admissible evolution.

Transition operators therefore represent the first candidate framework for recursive topology-preserving state dynamics in TIG.
