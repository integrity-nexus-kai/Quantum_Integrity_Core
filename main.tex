\begin{abstract}
Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) proposes a spectral-geometric framework 
for gravitational consistency. This work introduces the core formalism 
and explores its implications for black holes and cosmology.
\end{abstract}

% --------------------------------------------------
\section{Introduction}

General Relativity provides a geometrical description of gravitation in terms of spacetime curvature \cite{einstein1915}. 
While the theory is locally consistent and highly successful, it does not impose a fundamental global constraint on the admissibility of physical configurations.

Developments in black hole thermodynamics suggest that geometric structures encode deeper informational properties \cite{bekenstein1973, hawking1975}. 
Similarly, cosmological models describe large-scale dynamics with increasing observational precision, yet remain dependent on parameterized inputs rather than structural necessity \cite{friedmann1922}.

These observations indicate that current formulations of gravitational physics may be incomplete at the level of global consistency conditions.

In this work, we introduce Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG), a spectral-geometric framework that postulates a fundamental constraint on admissible physical configurations. 
Rather than modifying local dynamics, TIG imposes a global condition of topological integrity.

% --------------------------------------------------
\section{Topological Integrity Formalism}

We introduce the concept of topological integrity as a global constraint on admissible geometric configurations. 
Let $(M, g)$ be a smooth Lorentzian manifold representing spacetime.

We define an integrity functional $\mathcal{I}[g]$ that assigns a scalar measure of consistency to a given configuration.

In general form, we write:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{I}[g] = \int_M \mathcal{F}(R, \nabla R, \lambda_i, \dots)\, d\mu_g
\end{equation}

where $R$ denotes curvature invariants, $\nabla R$ their derivatives, and $\lambda_i$ represent spectral quantities.

Physical configurations satisfy an extremal condition:

\begin{equation}
\delta \mathcal{I}[g] = 0
\end{equation}

This defines a global consistency constraint on admissible spacetime geometries.

\section{Field Equations}

The extremal condition on the integrity functional leads to modified field equations extending General Relativity:

\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} + \mathcal{T}^{(\mathcal{I})}_{\mu\nu} = 8\pi T_{\mu\nu}
\end{equation}

where $\mathcal{T}^{(\mathcal{I})}_{\mu\nu}$ arises from the variation of the integrity functional.

In general:

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{T}^{(\mathcal{I})}_{\mu\nu} = \frac{\delta}{\delta g^{\mu\nu}} \int_M \mathcal{F}(R, \nabla R, \lambda_i, \dots)\, d\mu_g
\end{equation}

This term encodes global consistency constraints beyond local curvature dynamics.

\section{Black Hole Solutions}

We consider static, spherically symmetric solutions. 
In the classical limit, these are described by the Schwarzschild metric \cite{schwarzschild1916}.

With the integrity contribution, the vacuum equations become:

\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} + \mathcal{T}^{(\mathcal{I})}_{\mu\nu} = 0
\end{equation}

The integrity term introduces global constraints that may modify strong-field behavior.

Possible implications include:

\begin{itemize}
\item regularization of singularities,
\item constraints on horizon structure,
\item modified thermodynamic behavior.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}

This paper introduces a spectral-geometric framework for gravitational consistency. 
Topological Integrity Gravity imposes a global constraint on admissible spacetime configurations and provides a basis for further exploration of gravitational structure.
