\begin{abstract}
Topological Integrity Gravity (TIG) proposes a spectral-geometric framework 
for gravitational consistency. This work introduces the core formalism 
and explores its implications for black holes and cosmology.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Einstein's formulation of gravity \cite{einstein1915} provides the classical foundation.  
Modern developments suggest deeper connections between geometry and physical consistency.

\section{Core Idea}

We introduce the concept of topological integrity as a fundamental constraint 
on admissible physical configurations.

\section{Conclusion}

This paper outlines the foundational structure of TIG and motivates further exploration.
