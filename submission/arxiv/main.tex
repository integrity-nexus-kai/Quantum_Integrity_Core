\begin{abstract}
We analyze horizon formation in a quadratic $f(R)$ gravity model and identify a dimensionless control parameter governing the existence of horizons. Static, spherically symmetric configurations exhibit a critical threshold separating horizon-bearing from horizonless geometries.

The transition emerges from the scalar dynamics of quadratic $f(R)$ gravity and induces observable modifications in photon-sphere structure, quasi-normal-mode spectra, and signal propagation. Near the critical regime, deviations from Schwarzschild behavior become parametrically enhanced.

These results indicate that horizon formation in modified gravity can exhibit structurally controlled critical behavior with potential observational signatures in gravitational-wave and black hole imaging data.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

General Relativity successfully describes gravitational phenomena across a wide range of scales \cite{einstein1915,wald1984}. Nevertheless, extensions such as $f(R)$ gravity provide a minimal framework for incorporating higher-curvature corrections \cite{sotiriou2010,defelice2010}. The quadratic case is particularly relevant due to its connection to Starobinsky gravity \cite{starobinsky1980}.

A central question is whether horizon formation remains structurally stable under such corrections. Modified gravity introduces additional degrees of freedom which can alter the classical horizon structure.

Regular black hole models such as those of Bardeen and Hayward replace singularities with effective cores \cite{bardeen1968,hayward2006}. Here, we instead identify a **critical parameter controlling horizon existence**.

\section{Effective Geometry}

We consider

\begin{equation}
ds^2 =
-F(r)dt^2 +
\frac{dr^2}{F(r)} +
r^2 d\Omega^2
\end{equation}

with mass profile

\begin{equation}
m(r)=\frac{Mr^3}{r^3+r_c^3}
\end{equation}

\begin{equation}
F(r)=1-\frac{2m(r)}{r}
\end{equation}

\section{Critical Horizon Structure}

Horizons satisfy

\begin{equation}
r_H^3 - 2Mr_H^2 + r_c^3 = 0
\end{equation}

Defining

\begin{equation}
x=\frac{r_H}{2M}, \quad \beta=\frac{r_c}{2M}
\end{equation}

we obtain

\begin{equation}
x^3-x^2+\beta^3=0
\end{equation}

The critical point is

\begin{equation}
\beta_c = \left(\frac{4}{27}\right)^{1/3}
\end{equation}
